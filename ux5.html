IDENTITY & ACCESS MANAGEMENT (IAM)
CONTEXTUAL INQUIRY


Together with the University of Michigan's IAM team, our team of 5 graduate students analyzed and suggested potential solutions to enhance the Shibboleth configuration process. Shibboleth allows external cloud services the ability to authenticate and authorize pre-determined U-M users during log-in. The service provider must request a Shibboleth configuration to do this. This request on average can take 3 weeks up to 6 months to complete while the most successful request on a rare occasion was completed within 2 hours.

Contextual Inquiry is a user-centered design ethonographic research method where the researcher observes the user perform normal activities to understand their work behavior and style. Our analysis involved the following elements:

  • Background Research
  • User Interviews
  • Visual Work Models
  • Affinity Wall/Diagram
  • Technical Report
